# Competitor Analysis

**Last updated:** 23 July 2026
**Sources:** Reddit corpus (12,593 docs, provider mentions and sentiment), April keyword gap research, GTM positioning work
**Standing rule for comparison content:** be genuinely honest. Acknowledge where competitors are better. This earns EEAT, LLM citations, and conversions. The Reddit community's defining trait is distrust of promotion; dishonest comparisons burn the exact trust we're trying to build.

---

## The competitive map

TorchProxies sits between two tiers:

**Above us (enterprise):** Bright Data, Oxylabs. Full-stack data platforms, enterprise pricing, KYC-heavy onboarding.

**Direct mid-market competitors:** NodeMaven, RichProxies, BartProxies, Evomi. Named in company Priority 1 as the tier we need to break through.

**Below us:** budget providers competing purely on price with unreliable pools.

**Content competitors (not sales competitors):** Proxyway (the review authority everyone reads), Decodo, NodeMaven's blog. These set the content quality bar and are on the weekly RSS watch list.

## Per-competitor notes

### Bright Data
The most name-dropped provider in the proxy community (147 Reddit docs), consistently framed as the overpriced benchmark. That framing is exactly the flank we price against. Where they win: pool scale, tooling breadth (scraping APIs, datasets), enterprise compliance. Where we win: price at small-to-mid scale, no enterprise onboarding friction, pay-as-you-go with no contracts. The "TorchProxies vs Bright Data 2026" article (Q3 Week 2) is our first bottom-funnel piece; keep it honest enough that a Bright Data customer would agree with the characterization.

### NodeMaven
Closest positioning competitor in the mid-market residential space. Strong content operation, which makes them both a search competitor and the "alternative" query target (NodeMaven alternative 2026, KD 12, Q3 Week 7). Monitor their blog weekly.

### RichProxies / BartProxies
Named mid-market rivals. Low search volume on alternative queries (50-150/mo) but KD 8 and direct replacement intent. Both slotted in the Q3 calendar (Weeks 8 and 12).

### Evomi
Emerging budget-to-mid competitor, alternative query at KD 10. In the keyword library, not yet on the calendar.

### Oxylabs
Enterprise tier alongside Bright Data. Less community chatter than Bright Data. Not a current calendar target; relevant mostly as context in comparison tables.

## The content gap (our strategic openings)

1. **No mid-market provider produces no-code/AI-agent content.** Cluster 5 (n8n, MCP, AI agent infrastructure) is KD 5-10 with zero competition. We can own it within 60 days of publishing. This is the single clearest differentiator opening.
2. **Nobody in mobile does verifiable trust.** The community says no credible provider offers verifiably real carrier IPs and transparent pricing. Both are our Aug 2026 launch messaging.
3. **Honest bottom-funnel comparisons barely exist in the niche.** Most "X alternative" content is thinly veiled self-promotion. Genuinely honest comparisons are rare enough to be a format-level differentiator.
4. **Competitor SERP content is thin on technical depth.** The standard recon finding: top-5 results lack honest limitations, comparison tables, and exact thresholds. Our Layer 3 signals and specificity rules exist to exploit this.

## Per-article competitor recon (run for every article)

From the SOP, before any outline: search `[target keyword] 2026`, read the top 5 organic results, answer four questions: what structure do they use, what are they missing (technical depth? honest limitations? comparison table?), what does Google PAA show, what does "People Also Search For" show. The content gap is the winning angle.

## LLM citation competition (monthly audit)

Monthly, ask ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini the five benchmark questions (SNKRS proxies, best residential for scraping, Python requests proxies, ISP vs residential, LinkedIn automation proxy). Log whether TorchProxies appears; if a competitor is cited instead, identify what their cited article has that ours lacks and flag ours for update. Target: cited in 2 of 5 by Month 2, 5 of 5 by Month 4. ChatGPT.com already sends referral traffic; this channel is deliberate, not incidental.
