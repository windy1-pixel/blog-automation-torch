## ISP proxies: pool structure (critical, get this right)

ISP proxies are NOT a single generic pool. They are use-case-specific pools per country, each pre-tested for its target category. Never describe them as one undifferentiated pool.

**Countries (dashboard-confirmed May 2026):** USA, UK, Canada, Germany, Australia, Hong Kong, Korea, Japan.
Netherlands is NOT available. Neither are India or Indonesia. (An older SOP table listed NL/IT/FR; that is outdated. The dashboard is the source of truth.)

Writing rules that follow from this table:
- Social media articles: Social pool exists in US, UK, Germany only. Canada and HK readers get redirected to those or to residential.
- Sneaker articles: US, UK, Germany, Korea (Korea is sneakers-only).
- Ticketing: US, UK, Canada, Germany, Australia, Hong Kong.
- Retail: all 8 countries.
- Don't recommend Korea or Japan for social use cases.

Honest cross-sell in both directions between mobile and ISP ("residential pools are cooked, mobile is the escape" vs "static ISP does the same job cheaper" is the community's live debate; we enter it from both sides).

Do NOT use the old framing that deflected mobile-intent readers toward ISP. That was pre-launch strategy and is obsolete.
